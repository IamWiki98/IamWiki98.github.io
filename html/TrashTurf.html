Trash Turf

Download

This game was my fourth project at Breda University of Applied Sciences. I was assigned in Team 18 which consisted of 9 people. The team had 3 designers, 3 artists, and 3 programmers(me included). Three people of the team had designed the idea for the game before we were all assigned to it. I wasn't one of them so I joined later and we had 6 weeks to make a game using Unreal Engine 4. I worked on the Camera, Physics, Visual Effects, and UI since I found them to be most important.


Tasks

  • Spawn players for multiple controllers.
  • Make camera follow multiple players smoothly.
  • Make the raccoons be able to attack with their trash bags.
  • Add centrifugal force from the bag to the raccoon, depending on the size of the bag.
  • Add trash bag swinging, dependent on the strength of the raccoon.
  • Make players die when outside of camera vision.
  • Add visual effects for raccons getting hit or dying.
  • Add UI for the menu.

Camera

After I joined Team 18, I didn't know what to work on but I saw the camera as an important asset to the game. Consequently, I directly assigned myself to implement it. After experimenting with it, I relized that the game should be focused on the middle of the map and on the players at the same time. I figured I should make my own algorithm which tracks the players' location compared to the map's center location, and from there move the camera to a new location considering how close the players are to each other.


Physics

After making raccoons being able to hit each other, the lecturers advised us to implement physics in the game. I have studied physics in highschool so I immediately got interested and knew how to make the game feel with much better mechanics. I took centrifugal force into consideration since the game is literally raccoons swinging trash bags around themselves. I didn't research how centrifugal force works but instead created my own way of managing it and it turned out perfect. I then introduced raccoon strength and implemented the ability to swing the trash bag but using the force it already has applied to it. This means the raccoon can't quickly swing the bag just by standing still.


Visual Effects

I helped the artists to make the environnment feel more alive. I made looping clouds in the background. I made objects in front of the scene which move in respect of the camera so the player can feel as if they are in the scene. The artists wanted all the models to be paper-like. Consequently, I made the characters rotate as if they are made of paper. Unfortunately, the lecturers didn't like the idea so I removed it. Finally, I made an explosion effect when a raccoon goes out of the screen(dies).


UI

I somehow managed to create the whole menu in one night since an artist pushed me to do it before the game presentation. I was inexperienced with it but I achieved what is shown on the GIF. The different models are buttons.

This was the most enjoyable project to work on. Also, it was my best one when it comes to gameplay. Breda University has events for rewarding different games and Trash Turf was nominated 4 times which no other game could brag about with. Unfortunately, we didn't win any of the nominations but we definitely had an amazing game. Trash Turf was built in just 6 weeks time and we were with the smallest team. But yet again, the code is disastrous since we weren't that experienced.
